  1. Purpose of the study

    2. To understand public preference for urban green space as well as psychological impacts of nature exposure in order to help in designing landscape that could maximize public well-being as well as streamlining educational campaigns on environmental awareness.

  2. Participation and withdrawal

    3. Participation in this study is completely voluntary, and you may withdraw at any time without any negative consequences or penalty. If you wish to withdraw, simply stop completing the survey. Any data that is collected up to that point will not be used in the study.

  3. Confidentiality and security of data

    4. All data collected in this study will be stored confidentially and only members of the research team will have access to the data. Because all data is non-identifiable, responses cannot be linked to any individual participant. Data will be reported as group summaries and will only be used for the specific research purposes of this study.

  4. Risks

    5. Participation in this study should involve no physical or mental discomfort, and no risks beyond those of everyday living. However, should you feel uncomfortable answering any question, you may withdraw from participating in the study without any penalty or negative consequences.
